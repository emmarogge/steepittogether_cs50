Green Tea

About Green Tea

Green tea is the oldest form of tea, created from partially oxidized leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant. Green teas are generally light, bright, and grassy/earthy in flavor. Powdered green tea, known as matcha, has become very popular recently for its naturally sweet flavor and bright color.

Green teas have a moderate amount of caffeine (~30-50 mg per serving), but also contains a compound called L-Theanine, which may have a calming effect.

Brewing Instructions

Start heating up some water! Since green teas contain delicate flavors, it's best to use cooler water (between 160-180°F, or 75-85°C). This brewing temperature can be achieved by boiling water and allowing it to cool for around two minutes, or by using a kettle which allows for more fine-grain temperature control.

Loose leaf

Using a strainer or tea filter, measure out about one tablespoon of tea per 8 ounces of water. Pour the hot water over and let steep for 3 minutes.

Bagged

Simply put your teabag (or two if you'd like a stronger cup!) into your drinking vessel of choice, then fill the cup with water and let steep for 3 minutes.
