Herbal Tea

About Herbal Tea

Herbal teas, more formally known as tisanes, include any infusions which do not involve the Camellia sinensis plant. Many herbal teas have been used as folk-remedies: peppermint tea for a sore throat, ginger tea for an upset stomach, or chamomile tea for insomnia.

Many herbal teas are naturally caffeine-free, making them a perfect way to relax in the evening!

Brewing Instructions

Start heating up some water! Most herbal teas should be brewed using boiling water to maximally extract flavor.

Loose leaf

Using a strainer or tea filter, measure out about one tablespoon of tea per 8 ounces of water. Pour the hot water over and let steep for 5 minutes.

Bagged

Simply put your teabag (or two if you'd like a stronger cup!) into your drinking vessel of choice, then fill the cup with water and let steep for 5 minutes.
